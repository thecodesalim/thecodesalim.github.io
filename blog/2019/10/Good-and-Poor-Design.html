Brief

In my Resarch and Communication class, I was given tasked with producing 3 good and 3 poor designs using the 10 Principle of Good Design by Dieter Rams.

Research

It is important to understand why good design is important. Design is a form of communication, communications are meant to be clear and easy to understand. Same applies for good design.

What is good design? That is where Dieter Rams takes control. Dieter Rams is a German Industrial Designer known for his ‚Äúless is better‚Äù philosophy which he used to design several products for Braun. He led and laid guidelines for the Functionalist design movement. Functionalist design is a design movement in which the form of the object is driven by the purpose of the object and not by its aesthetics.

Dieter Rams codified his learning experience as a Designer into a set of 10 collective principles.

The 10 Principles of Good Design

  1. Good design is innovative
  2. Good design makes a product useful
  3. Good design is aesthetic
  4. Good design makes a product understandable
  5. Good design is unobtrusive
  6. Good design is honest
  7. Good design is long lasting
  8. Good design is thorough down to the last detail
  9. Good design is environmentally friendly
  10. Good design is as little design as possible

Good Design

  1. Tote Bag

A Tote Bag (see Fig. 1) ticks all the 10 Good Design Principles by Dieter Rams. A tote bag design is simple and allows the user to use in anyway or form. The user can hold it, put on it on their shoulder or strap it around. The design makes it obvious what it is made for.

Poor Design

  1. Switch Wall

I have a thing about switches and they are overused to the extent they are in almost every building and people are accustomed to it. At the apartment I live in I have the switch wall (see fig. 1). The switch wall has the switches for the Cooker, Laundry Machine, Fan and Oven. The big issue is you cannot tell which switch is for which appliance without actually switching it on.

As Dieter Rams listed in his design principles a design should be unobtrusive, makes the product understandable but this wall switch fails miserably on those design principles. Most people will blame themselves saying ‚ÄúI don‚Äôt how it works‚Äù. A good design should eliminate the need for that an automatically informs the users what each switch does. This is a small wall switch imagine an auditorium having such, it will be a complete disaster.

They are two way this problem can be fixed:

1. Leaving the switch on the appliances
2. Label each switch

  1. Snapchat

Snapchat a mobile phone app started out as an app that allows users to exchange disappearing photos. With time snapchat kept adding features that made the app lose most of its user base.

These features added include text messaging, phone calls, video calls and also building a gaming platform into the app. The term used for this, is called ‚Äúfeature creeping‚Äù . Feature creep is the excessive ongoing expansion or addition of new features in a product, especially in computer software, video games and consumer and business electronics. These extra features go beyond the basic function of the product and can result in software bloat and over-complication, rather than simple design.

Good design makes a product understandable, snapchat design is not that. A new user coming to use Snapchat will be overwhelmed by the features the app provide, which makes the application difficult to understand and use.
